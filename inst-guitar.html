BIO

STEFANOS KOUTELIS

Stefanos Koutelis (born Athens, 1984) is a Greek/Cypriot musician: guitarist, composer/arranger.

He started learning guitar at the age of 10 and doublebass five years later joining the Cyprus National Youth Symphonic Orchestra.

In 2002 he began his musical studies at the University of Pecs in Hungary, majoring in classical guitar performance. In 2005 he returned to Cyprus to work, while continuing his studies at the University of Nicosia. The final recital exam was in 2007, where he passed with honors, graduating with distinction (cum laude). He participated in numerous classical concerts in Hungary and Cyprus as a soloist, or in a guitar trio setting (chamber music), or as a double bassist with the university’s symphonic orchestra.

He has been a member of multiple well established bands (Amnisia, Jambulance, Big Band Theory, The Scordions) performing various musical styles (rock, funk, blues, jazz, classical, traditional etc). He participated in countless concerts nationwide with bands, along with famous Greek artists (Christos Thiveos, Kitrina Podilata, Ypogeia Revmata, Antonis Mitzelos, Pavlos Pavlidis, Thanos Anestopoulos, Manos Xydous, Miltiadis Paschalides, Magic de Spell, Antigoni Katsouri, Dimitris Baslam etc) and appeared several times on television.

He attended electric,classical guitar and other music seminars with master musicians (Michael Sadanowsky, Gary Ryan, Doug Aldrich, Yiotis Kiourtsoglou, Stavros Lantsias, Notis Mavroudis, Antonia Forrari etc).

Since 2005 he works as a teacher of electric, acoustic and classical guitar either freelance or in collaboration with music schools (DM essence of Art, Ethniko Odeio, Pindario Odeio, Europaiko Odeio, CG Georgallis Music House, String Theory music school). He has also worked performing live, guitar or bass in a regular basis in almost all of Cyprus’ music venues.

Since 2010 he owns a recording studio, working with various projects and artists. As side projects he is involved in programming and video shooting/editing & animation.
